CPUs

The Central Processing Unit (CPU)is the brain of the computer. Everything that happens within the PC goes through the CPU. All computers have CPU's, for a PC to function a CPU is always necessary. To build a PC, there are two main players for CPU's, AMD's RYZEN® lineup of CPU's, Or Intel's Core® lineup of CPU's. Both brands are a good choice, but they fit into different sockets on the motherboard, so after choosing a CPU, it is important to make sure your motherboard has the appropriate socket. Most of these CPUs come with a stock heatsink to keep the CPU at a good temperature, these heatsinks work fine and you only need to get a different one if you plan to overclock. CPU's also have cores and threads. Cores are the hardware specifications, individual processors within the CPU that make up the CPU. Threads are software related specifications, how many programming sequences can the processor do at once. We have a list of the best CPUs from both companies depending on your budget, and what you plan to do on the PC.

Mid-range processors

The RYZEN® 5 series of CPU's are great affordable CPUs that performs really well. They are good for gaming and video editing. There is the 1600, 2600, 3600, 1600x, 2600x and 3600x. All of these perform a little different, the x’s being better, but overall are good processors.

On the Intel side of things, you have the i5 series of processors. There are a lot of them which you can find here. The newer versions of the processor with the most cores and threads will be the best.

The Ryzen 7 and 9’s are on the higher end, and can perform most, if not all tasks you could think of on a PC.

Intel has the Core® i7 and i9 series, which are very simular to the RYZEN® 7 and 9 processors except for one key differance. The i9 procesors have Intel Quick Sync®, which intregrates really well with the Adobe Creative Cloud Suite of applications. In fact, if you plan to use any Adobe programs, Intel is probally the best option for you, because an Intel processor with fewer cores will outpreform a RYZEN® processor with more cores, because of Intel Quick Sync®. This is important especially if you are using Premiere Pro® or Photoshop®, which require a lot of processing power.

Ryzen also have special processors: For AMD Ryzen, there is the threadripper, and the xeon for intel. These are expensive and are only necessary if you plan on really pushing your PC.

All of the cpu’s listed a good CPUs for any system that are reliable and will bring good performance.
